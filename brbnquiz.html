Michter's: Small Batch
Nose: Warm aromas of baked goods dominate — vanilla, cinnamon, apples, and brown butter evoke a streusel dessert. Rich caramel rounds out the sweet, inviting profile.

Palate: A touch of rye spice and cinnamon heat adds depth. The sweetness from the nose evolves into caramel candies and toasted citrus zest, with just a hint of oak peeking through.

Finish: Medium in length, offering more toasted orange zest, cinnamon, and clove. Rye spice lingers pleasantly, adding complexity to an otherwise sweet and smooth close.

Score: 5.7/10
Rabbit Hole - Heigold
Nose: A pleasant scent of toasted caramel, toffee, and a bit of butterscotch. All are scents any bourbon enthusiast would like. Vanilla custard and a faint amount of oak show some nice barrel notes, but can’t entirely cover up the toasted grains scent. It’s not really grainy, but it doesn’t exactly smell aged either. There is a noticeable lack of fruit and aged scents like leathers and tobaccos, as one might expect with a 3-year age statement. Overall, a very pleasant nose that lacks an exciting dynamic.

Palate: The mouthfeel comes off creamier than expected. Sweetness is found everywhere in sufficient quantities to mostly cover up a bit of youthful astringency and graininess still hiding in the background. Cherries and apricots can be found, and there is a bit of cinnamon spice and even oak spice lurking here and there. Some of the baking spices are soft while others are a bit raw and come off as sharp. As the sip goes on, an element of wintergreen mint comes out and the stone fruits seem to overtake the sweetness.

Finish: For the youthful age statement, there are more tannins than expected. Dry wood and barrel char follow heavily caramelized stone fruit. The finish has a slight herbaceousness in the end, with mint sticking out the most. This does not ruin the overall sweetness, but is interesting nonetheless.

Score: 6/10
Uncle Nearest - Single Barrel
Nose: Sweet, nutty aromas of marzipan, caramel, and vanilla wafers open the experience. Roasted corn-on-the-cob and cinnamon spice follow, with subtle floral notes and a touch of dry hay in the background.

Palate: Rich and rounded. Flavors include roasted corn, marzipan vanilla, new leather, and oak. Fruit character arrives through cherries, orange zest, grape soda, and even a hint of Flintstone chewable vitamins. Caramel turns into wildflower honey, chased by a splash of ginger beer.

Finish: Grain-forward and lingering. Think hoecakes topped with vanilla icing, almond skins replacing the earlier marzipan, and tannins reminiscent of wood polish. Cherries and bitter orange persist with a surprising whisper of banana at the end.

Score: 7.6/10
Old Forester: The 117 Series
Mash Bill: 65% Rye, 20% Malted Barley, 15% Corn
Age: 10 years (Bottled-In-Bond)

Nose: Layered notes of chocolate, graham crackers, and vanilla lead the way, joined by well-aged oak bordering on antique. Sweetness from brown sugar and macchiato blends with subtle campfire and cedar cigar box aromas. A hint of floral character rounds it out.

Palate: Rich and complex. Brownie edges, toasted coconut, cooked pear, and agave syrup unfold into black cherry, hazelnut spread, and French vanilla. Tannic elements like cigar wrapper and seasoned oak hint at greater age. Rye spice reveals itself with notes of menthol and cinnamon.

Finish: Vanilla and brown sugar fade into cinnamon, nutmeg, and poached orchard fruits. Coconut shell and toffee linger gently.

Score: 8.3/10
